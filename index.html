📄 DOCUMENT — Market Analysis Report

DealForce B2B SaaS — Internal Market Report Q3


DealForce has been operating in the B2B software sector for three years. The company targets small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) looking for affordable customer relationship management (CRM) solutions. Unlike its competitors, DealForce positions itself as a flexible, low-cost option with a strong focus on onboarding support.


However, recent data suggests a worrying trend. Customer acquisition costs (CAC) have risen by 34% over the past two quarters, while the conversion rate from free trial to paid subscription has dropped to 12%. The average sales cycle has lengthened from 18 days to 31 days.


The marketing team believes the problem lies in a mismatch between the product's value proposition and the messaging used in cold outreach. Many prospects report that they were not fully aware of DealForce's automation features, which are considered a key differentiator.


The sales director recommends a full audit of the pitch deck, a retraining programme for all SDRs (Sales Development Representatives), and an overhaul of the LinkedIn prospecting strategy. The board expects a revised commercial action plan to be submitted within 30 days.
